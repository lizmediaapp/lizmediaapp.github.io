Video Call Jitter

What is good jitter for video calls?

Video calls usually work best when jitter stays low and consistent. Even if your internet speed is high, unstable latency can create robotic audio, delayed speech, frozen frames, and dropped meeting quality.

A practical jitter target for calls

Jitter Call experience
Below 10 ms Usually stable and smooth for most meetings.
10 to 20 ms Often acceptable, but occasional distortions may happen.
Above 20 ms More likely to cause audio breakup, visual freezing, and awkward conversation timing.

Signs high jitter is affecting calls

  • Audio sounds metallic or broken.
  • People talk over each other because timing feels off.
  • Video freezes even when the meeting reconnects quickly.
  • Call quality changes minute to minute instead of staying stable.

How to improve jitter for calls

  • Prefer Ethernet or stronger Wi-Fi coverage.
  • Stop cloud sync, backups, and large downloads during meetings.
  • Test near the router and compare with your usual work location.
  • Use repeated tests to see whether the issue is time-based congestion.
